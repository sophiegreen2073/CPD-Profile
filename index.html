About Me

My name is Sophie Green, I am a 25 years old and studying MSc Robotics at the University of Manchester. I have a BSc in Mathematics and Computer Science also from the University of Manchester, completed in 2022. I spent three years working as a Software Developer, focusing on warehouse automation and simulation at a manufacturing company. I have an interest in robotics in extreme environments, focusing on land and water robots in particular.

Skills

  • Programming in Python, C#, HTML, Javascript, AngularJS, MySQL and ROS2.
  • Creating WPFs for operator control on manufacturing lines.
  • Creating simulations using Emulate3D.
  • Writing MES solutions that handshake with PLCs for controlling elements of automation within warehouses (including cobots, vision systems etc.).
  • Working in teams with control engineers, designers and mechanical engineers to reach a common goal.
  • MoveIt2, Gazebo and RViz2 for manipulator control.

    Projects

    Projects I have contributed to in work and at university. For further information on projects 1, 2 and 4 please contact Ian.Colledge@ga-petfoodpartners.co.uk.

    1: Automated Labelling Line

    I created a MES system for an automated labelling line for full bags of pet food. This was a bespoke solution, incorporating Universal Robots, cameras with build in deep learning models and PLCs for controlling the individual components.

    2: Manufacturing and Distribution Warehouse Simulation

    I am working on an ongoing simulation of an automated packing hall for packing dry pet food into bags. This includes simulating the new version of the distribution warehouse. I used Emulate3D, reading CAD designs and outputting throughput numbers to support the design process.

    3: Leo Rover Object Retrieval

    In a dynamic team as part of my master's degree we built a Leo Rover with a manipulator arm to retrieve coloured blocks and place them in a bin of the same colour. This requires using ROS and RViz to control the Rover and Manipulator arm. My key role in this project was control of the manipulator arm, integrating MoveIt2 to determine pick up and drop movements.

    4: Quality Assurance Vision System

    Alongside KPM I integrated a vision system that uses two lasers to measure individual kibble. These measurements are then used to approve/reject bacthes as they go in to be packed and are being fixed to every line.
